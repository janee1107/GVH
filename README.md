## What is This?

The **GVH 2026 Campaign Website** is the public-facing promotional site for MediaTek's *Global Volunteer Hero Recognition* (GVH) — a company-wide CSR award program honoring employees who contribute to their communities through regular volunteering.

This was the compant internal **first website built entirely through vibe coding** — writing all product requirements, copy, and UI specifications through iterative AI dialogue with no prior coding background.

---

## What It Does

The site serves as the full program hub for 15,000+ global MediaTek employees:

- **Program introduction** — mission, values, and what "The Unseen Hero" means
- **Eligibility & categories** — volunteering types that qualify (community care, skills-based, eco, animal welfare)
- **Award benefits** — USD $3,000 charity donation grant, Sustainability Ambassador recognition
- **Event timeline** — key milestones from nomination open to award ceremony
- **FAQ** — 17 questions covering eligibility, process, judging criteria, and results
- **Application CTA** — direct link to Jarvis, the AI writing agent that helps employees draft their volunteer story

---

## The Jarvis Integration

A central feature of the site is its integration with **Jarvis**, MediaTek's internal AI Copilot (built on Microsoft Copilot Studio). The site's primary CTA points employees to a custom Jarvis agent that:

1. Guides applicants through a structured conversation
2. Extracts key details about their volunteer activities
3. Generates a polished application story on their behalf

This removed the biggest barrier to participation: **"I don't know how to write my story."**

---

## Design Philosophy

The site was designed around the campaign concept **"Small Act, Big Impact"** — warm, human, and emotionally resonant rather than corporate.

Key design decisions:
- **Bilingual**: Seamless EN / 中 language toggle for global and Taiwan-based employees
- **Conversational tone**: FAQ written to feel like a helpful colleague, not a legal document
- **Low-friction CTA**: "Apply with Jarvis" appears multiple times, always with clear context
- **Volunteer category cards**: Visual categorization to help employees self-identify
